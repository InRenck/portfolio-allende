About Me

I'm Allende Renck and Art critic

Graduated in Literature from the Federal University of Santa Catarina, with a Master's degree in Poetry and Aisthesis from the Graduate Program in Literature at UFSC, and currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Modernity Theory in the same program. In their doctoral research, they explore the history of Black Art as a form of political resistance within the Afro-diasporic context in the Americas. They are the creator and host of the YouTube channel and Podcast 'Complexo Arte-Política,' where they discuss cultural topics from the perspective of critical theory and its relationship with the political organization of society. They have co-organized the book 'Cidades Rebeldes: Invisibilidades, Silenciamentos, Resistências e Potências' alongside anthropologists Paulo Raposo and Scott Head, and contribute critical texts on artistic genres and cultural phenomena to various publications. Additionally, they work in curating exhibitions, festivals, and shows, and offer courses and lectures on Art, Culture, Politics, Psychoanalysis, Ethnic-racial Relations, and related subjects.
